Suya (Spicy Grilled Skewers)

Suya is a popular and spicy street food from West Africa, particularly Nigeria. It consists of thinly sliced meat, typically beef, which is marinated in a complex spice mix called Yaji, threaded onto skewers, and grilled to perfection.

Ingredients

  • 500g beef sirloin, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup Yaji (Suya spice mix)
  • 2 tbsp peanut oil or vegetable oil
  • Wooden skewers, soaked in water
  • For serving: sliced onions, tomatoes, and cabbage

Instructions

Show cooking steps
  1. In a large bowl, toss the sliced beef with the oil until well-coated.
  2. Generously coat each slice of beef with the Yaji spice mix, pressing it in to adhere.
  3. Thread the seasoned meat onto the soaked wooden skewers.
  4. Grill over medium-high heat for 3-5 minutes per side, until cooked through and slightly charred.
  5. Serve immediately with fresh onions, tomatoes, and extra Yaji for dipping.

Country of Origin

Suya originated with the Hausa people of Northern Nigeria and has spread across West Africa.
