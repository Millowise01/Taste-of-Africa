Ugali

Ugali is a staple food in many East African countries. It's a stiff porridge made from maize flour (cornmeal), cooked in boiling water or milk until it reaches a firm, dough-like consistency. It's the perfect accompaniment to stews and grilled meats.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups water
  • 2 cups white maize flour (cornmeal), plus more if needed

Instructions

Show cooking steps
  1. Bring the water to a boil in a large pot.
  2. Gradually add the maize flour while stirring continuously with a wooden spoon to prevent lumps.
  3. Continue to stir and cook, adding more flour as needed, until the mixture thickens and pulls away from the sides of the pot (about 10-15 minutes).
  4. Shape the ugali into a mound and reduce the heat to low. Cover and let it steam for a few minutes.
  5. Turn the ugali out onto a plate. It can be served immediately, hot.

Country of Origin

Ugali is a dietary staple across East Africa, particularly in Kenya, Tanzania, and Uganda.
