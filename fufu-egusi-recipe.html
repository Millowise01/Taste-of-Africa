Fufu & Egusi Soup

A quintessential West African dish, Egusi Soup is a hearty soup made from ground melon seeds and leafy vegetables. It's often served with a starchy side like Fufu, a soft, pliable dough made from cassava or yams.

Ingredients (for Egusi Soup)

  • 1 cup ground egusi (melon seeds)
  • 500g beef or assorted meat, cut into pieces
  • 1/4 cup palm oil
  • 2 red bell peppers
  • 1 scotch bonnet pepper
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 tbsp ground crayfish
  • 1 bunch spinach or bitter leaf, chopped
  • Stock cubes and salt to taste

Instructions

Show cooking steps
  1. Boil the meat with onion and stock cubes until tender.
  2. Blend bell peppers and scotch bonnet. Heat palm oil in a separate pot, add the pepper mix, and fry for 10 minutes.
  3. Mix ground egusi with a little water to form a thick paste. Add spoonfuls to the pot and fry for a few minutes.
  4. Add the boiled meat and its stock, ground crayfish, and salt. Stir gently and let it simmer for 15 minutes.
  5. Add the chopped spinach or bitter leaf, cook for another 5 minutes, and serve hot with Fufu.

Country of Origin

This dish is widely popular across West Africa, especially in Nigeria.
