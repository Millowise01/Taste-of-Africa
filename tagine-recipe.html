Moroccan Tagine

A tagine is a North African stew named after the earthenware pot in which it is cooked. It's known for its aromatic blend of sweet and savory flavors, often featuring meat, poultry, or fish along with vegetables or fruit. Spices like cinnamon, saffron, and ginger are key.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg lamb shoulder or chicken thighs
  • 2 onions, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 tsp turmeric, 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp cinnamon
  • Pinch of saffron threads
  • 1 preserved lemon, quartered
  • 1 cup green olives
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 2 tbsp olive oil

Instructions

Show cooking steps
  1. Heat olive oil in the tagine base. Brown the meat on all sides and set aside.
  2. Sauté onions, garlic, and ginger until soft. Add the spices and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  3. Return the meat to the pot. Add the broth, saffron, and preserved lemon.
  4. Cover with the conical lid and simmer on low heat for 1.5-2 hours, or until the meat is tender.
  5. Add the olives for the last 15 minutes of cooking. Serve hot with couscous.
