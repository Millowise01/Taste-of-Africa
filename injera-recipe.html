Injera & Stews

Injera is a sour, spongy, fermented flatbread from Ethiopia and Eritrea. It's made from teff flour, a tiny, ancient grain. It serves as both the plate and the utensil for scooping up various flavorful stews (wats).

Ingredients (for Injera)

  • 1 cup teff flour
  • 1.5 cups lukewarm water
  • Pinch of salt

Note: Authentic injera requires a 2-3 day fermentation process.

Instructions

Show cooking steps
  1. Mix teff flour with water in a bowl until a smooth batter forms.
  2. Cover with a cloth and let it ferment in a warm place for 2-3 days, until bubbly and sour-smelling.
  3. Stir in the salt. The batter should be thin, like a crepe batter.
  4. Lightly oil a large non-stick skillet over medium heat. Pour a thin layer of batter to cover the bottom.
  5. Cook for 1-2 minutes, until bubbles form and the edges lift. Do not flip.
  6. Carefully remove the injera and let it cool. Serve with your favorite stews (wats).
