Nyama Choma

Nyama Choma, which means "grilled meat" in Swahili, is a beloved dish in East Africa, especially Kenya. It's more than just a meal; it's a social event where friends and family gather around a grill. The meat, typically goat or beef, is slow-roasted over an open fire.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg goat meat, on the bone, cut into large chunks
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Optional marinade: garlic, ginger, lemon juice
  • For serving: Kachumbari salad (tomatoes, onions, cilantro)

Instructions

Show cooking steps
  1. If using a marinade, rub it all over the meat and let it sit for at least an hour.
  2. Prepare a charcoal grill for medium-low heat.
  3. Season the meat generously with salt and pepper.
  4. Place the meat on the grill and cook slowly, turning occasionally, for 45-60 minutes or until cooked through and tender.
  5. Let the meat rest for a few minutes before carving it off the bone into bite-sized pieces.
  6. Serve immediately with Ugali and Kachumbari.

Country of Origin

Nyama Choma is a national dish of Kenya and a celebrated part of its culinary culture.
